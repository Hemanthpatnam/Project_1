Best Destinations for Nature Lovers in India

*Although, this country is full of natural hotspots, which constitutes beaches, snow-capped mountains, deserts, dense forests, and waterfalls. The description of the natural beauty of India is given in various ancient books too. Let’s jump straight on the list of best destinations for nature lovers in India.

List of the Top 13 Destinations for Nature Lovers in India:

The natural beauty of India is appraisable, whether be the snow-capped mountains of Himachal, beaches of Goa, Sundarbans of Bengal, or the desert of Rajasthan. Here are the most popular destinations for nature lovers.

1. Majuli, Assam

Majuli is a stunning island, located in Assam. In India, it is the first Island to become a district. It hides nature’s beauty in itself, as it surrounds itself with two great rivers. One is the largest river named the Brahmaputra and the second one is the Subansiri River. It covers approximately 553 square kilometers of area.
Countless endangered species live there. Especially migratory birds including Adjutant Strokes, Siberian crane, and Pelican. The best time to visit Majuli is in February.

2. Sundarbans Forest, West Bengal

The great Sundarbans is a mangrove area in Bengal and is the only largest Bengal Tiger reserve, in the whole of India. This is present in the delta formed by the Brahmaputra and Meghna River. The ecosystem of this Biosphere reserve works fine and maintains the rich habitat accordingly.

Here you will get a chance to see diverse species like brown-winged kingfishers, olive ridley turtles, fishing cats, dog-faced water snakes, mudskipper, sea snakes, and swamp tiger.

3. Kerala Backwaters

The backwaters in Kerala are magnetic, hence attracting tourists from all across the world. These waters flow parallel to the Arabian seacoasts and many interconnected canals, rivers, and inlets. You will find rich flora and fauna in these chains of brackish lagoons and lakes.

As this is different from any other water body, hence it comprises a unique ecosystem for the specie living there. These brackish lagoons are spread over 900 kilometers.
